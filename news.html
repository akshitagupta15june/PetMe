Laika was the first dog in space.

Laika was a stray dog who was found on the streets of Moscow in 1954. She was chosen to be the first dog in space because she was small and healthy. Laika was launched into space on board Sputnik 2 on November 3, 1957. She died in orbit a few days later due to overheating.

Hachiko was a loyal dog who waited for his owner for 10 years.

Hachiko was a Japanese Akita who was born in 1923. He was given to Hidesaburo Ueno, a professor at Tokyo University, in 1924. Hachiko would greet Ueno every day at the train station when he came home from work and would wait for him to return at night. On May 25, 1925, Ueno died of a cerebral hemorrhage at work. Hachiko did not know that his owner was not coming home, and he continued to wait for him at the train station every day for the next 10 years until his own death in 1935.

Balto led a team of sled dogs that delivered diphtheria medicine to Nome, Alaska.

In 1925, a diphtheria epidemic broke out in Nome, Alaska. There was no way to get the medicine to the town by plane or boat, so a team of sled dogs was called in. Balto was the leader of the team, and he led them through a blizzard to deliver the medicine. The dogs were hailed as heroes, and Balto was given a parade in New York City.

Strongheart was a movie star dog who appeared in over 20 films.

Strongheart was a German Shepherd who was born in 1917. He was trained by the American Humane Association, and he appeared in over 20 films. Strongheart was one of the most popular movie stars of the silent film era, and he helped to change the public's perception of dogs.

Meet Pearl, the pocket-sized chihuahua named shortest dog by Guinness World Records

Measuring at 3.59 inches tall and just five inches in length, Pearl is shorter than a popsicle stick and around the same size as a dollar bill, according to the Guinness World Records. When the chihuahua was born in September 2020 at Crystal Creek Animal Hospital in Orlando, Florida, she weighed less than an ounce at birth.
